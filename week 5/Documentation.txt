
OOP Week 5 Lab Documentation - Encapsulation Q&A

2.1. Why are the variables declared as private?

Variables are declared private to enforce data hiding. This prevents 
external classes or unauthorized users from directly accessing or modifying 
the internal state of an object, protecting its integrity.

2.2. What happens if the variables are declared public?

If variables are public, any external class can bypass the safety controls
and change them directly (e.g., setting a student's CGPA to -5.0 or 99.0). 
This violates the principle of encapsulation and makes the application 
vulnerable to data corruption and bugs.

2.3. Why do we use getters and setters?

Getters and setters act as controlled access points (mediators) for private 
variables. 
- Getters grant safe, read-only access to the data.
- Setters control how data is changed, allowing us to include validation 
  logic (like ensuring a CGPA stays between 0.0 and 4.0) before updating the 
  internal variable.
